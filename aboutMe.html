Hello! My name is Noatak Post!

I was born in Kotzebue, Alaska, but spent most of my life growing up in Juneau, Alaska. In grade school I was incredibly focused on music and robotics. I play the viola and the violin and in my freshmna year of high school I was invited to join the Juneau Symphony Orchestra. Throughout my entire youth I was involved with the FIRST robotics program. Originally through their FIRST Lego League (FLL) program, and then later the FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC). In FTC I was the captain of my team for three years, and in my third year as captain, my team became the Alaska State Champions. We then went on to compete at the World Championships for that season.

In college, I have been attending Pacific Lutheran University (PLU) in Tacoma, Washington, where I am currently in my fourth year, working on completing a double degree program. My two degrees are a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science and a Bachelor of Muic in Music Education. I started school with my music degree, so as of now (October 2022) I am nearing the end of my required classes for my music degree, and I am just starting to dive head on into the world of computer science. I expect to graduate in the December of 2024, after I have completed my computer science course work, and my music student-teaching.

I am especially passionate about working in the video game industry after I have finished school. I see Video Games as the ultimate culmiation of nearly all art forms, which also include a level of interaction and engagement with it's audience that no other form of art or entertainment is able to achieve. Being a musician I am also espcially interested in the way that music is used in games to convey a narrative and portray a world to the audience. Some of my biggest inspirations in terms of game design, narrative, and music in no particular order include Journey (thatgamecompany), ABZU (Giant Squid), The Pathless (Giant Squid), The Beginner's Guide (Davey Wreden), Outer Wilds (Mobius Digital), Stardew Valley (ConcernedApe), Homeworld (Relic Entertainment), Subsurface Circular (Bithell Games), and No Man's Sky (Hello Games).

In my free time I have a number of games that I am working on as personal projects. These projects include byt are not limited to:

- Multi-Dimensional Tic-Tac-Toe

An attempt to make the classic game of Tic-Tac-Toe more engaging to play and more challenging to win. (Currently playable under the projects section of this website!)

- Jig

A pixel-art fishing game where each fish you hook starts a Guitar Hero-style rhythm game that you must win to catch the fish. This is a project I am working on with a friend.

- Invader Evader

An Oculus Quest 2 game where the player pilots a spaceship using the hand-tracking on the Quest 2 to avoid incoming astroids and alien attackers in an endless-runner style loop.

- Conductor VR

An idea for my Computer Science Senior Capstone project that incorperates the skills I have acquired from both my Computer Science and Music degrees as well as experience gained making Jig and Invader Evader. Condctor VR is a virtual practice room for students to learn and practice the art of conducting a music ensemble. Inspired by the lack of access and accomodations available to conducting students during the COVID-19 Pandemic, Condctor VR aims to create a way for conducting teachers and students to engage eachother in realtime in a 3D environment from anywhere in the world. Additionally it will offer practice tools to help conducting students improve the positioning and technique of their conducting through visual and audio assitance.
